What Resilience Is

Resilience is the ability to rebuild and grow from adversity. It is a trait that every human is capable of developing. For children, the key for developing resilience is having caring and supportive relationships with adults

Social and Emotional Learning

Social and emotional learning (SEL) is the process through which people acquire and effectively apply the knowledge, attitudes, and skills necessary to understand and manage emotions, set and achieve positive goals, feel and show empathy for others, establish and maintain positive relationships, and make responsible decisions. SEL skills are necessary for effective life functioning. Developing SEL skills is also strongly linked to developing resiliency.

Tips for Helping Students Develop SEL Skills

  • Promote and encourage self-reflection.
  • Encourage students to regularly share what they are learning in school with their parents.
  • Help students learn from their mistakes and emphasize that making mistakes is a crucial part of learning.
  • Explicitly teach what resilience is and how it can be developed.
  • Provide meaningful opportunities for students to collaborate and share their ideas and opinions with each other.
  • Build supportive relationships with students.
  • Set high, yet realistic, expectations for students.

Classroom Activities that Promote Resilience

  • Read and discuss relatable stories in which the characters overcome challenges.
  • Learn about historic figures who faced adversity and have students discuss what they would feel like in those situations and what they could do to overcome the challenges.
  • Have students keep journals in which they write about challenges they are facing and how they are planning to overcome them.
  • Have students learn about mistakes that happened in various contexts and discuss the positive things that came from those mistakes.
  • Provide math or science questions and have students create as many incorrect answers as possible. Have the students discuss what they can learn from each incorrect answer.
