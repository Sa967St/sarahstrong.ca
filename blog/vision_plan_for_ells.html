Vision

  • make ELLs feel welcomed and comfortable at school

  • make ELLs feel that their cultural and linguistically background is valued

  • help reduce challenges ELLs face in the classroom due to language barriers

  • make ELLs feel capable of learning and accomplishing things

  • provide ELLs with learning opportunities that are meaningful and allow for the development of higher-order thinking skills

Orientation Process

  • ensure they know the location of the office, all their classrooms, washrooms, cafeteria, gym, locker, etc.

  • ensure familiarity of classroom and school rules

  • ensure familiarity of school schedule and bell times

  • ensure familiarity of emergency procedures

  • ensure they know where to go for help

  • introduce them to support staff

  • find and introduce them to staff that speak their language

Welcoming Classroom Environment

  • multi-lingual messages

  • character posters

  • safe space posters

  • show them that making mistakes is part of learning

  • show them that they can come to me for support

Initial Assessment

  • assess prior knowledge using a diagnostic assessment that is straight forward and easy to comprehend
    • use simple and minimalistic language
    • incorporate visuals when appropriate
    • allow responses that go beyond what the questions are asking

  • take-up the assessment together
    • reassure that their test result is not a measure of their worth, it's simply used to find gaps in learning that are natural when transferring between schools
    • discuss how they felt during the assessment
    • find out whether anything caused confusion

Integration of Culture Into Lessons

  • provide opportunities for ELLs to incorporate their culture into their assignments

  • use scenarios using aspects of different cultures when coming up with practice problems

  • avoid using examples involving Canadian or American culture without ensuring that they understand them

Differentiated Instruction and Activity

  • provide both written and oral instructions for tasks

  • provide list of new terminology ahead of time, with room to translate and write/draw examples and definitions

  • provide room on worksheets for translations and personal notes

  • allow students to express their solutions to problems in various forms (e.g. diagrams, equations, paragraphs, etc.)

Other

  • communicate regularly with the ESL teacher to discuss the students' progress and strategies that are helpful
