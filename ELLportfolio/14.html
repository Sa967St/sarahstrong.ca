Response to "Strategy Inventory for Language Learning"

Strategy Inventory for Language Learning is a survey that assesses language learning strategies based on these six categories: memory, cognitive, compensatory, social, affective, and metacognitive. Here is my response to taking this survey (replacing every instance of "English" with the languages I am currently learning), which is a part of a journal entry.


In which of the six categories did you score the highest? Lowest?

My answers varied depending on the language. I'm learning several languages right now and I have different goals for some of them. My goals are to become fluent in speaking Japanese and Esperanto and in reading Italian and French. I scored the highest in cognitive and social for Japanese and Esperanto, and in memory and compensatory for Italian and French. I scored the lowest in affective for all of them.


What were your predominant language learning strategies?

  • Connect the sound or spelling of a new word with an image of it.
  • Use flashcards to drill word meanings and spellings.
  • Memorize common phrases and expressions.
  • Watch TV shows in different languages with subtitles in that language.
  • Make educated guesses when unfamiliar words are encountered.
  • Take notice of mistakes and how to correct them.
  • Ask questions about the language to native speaker.

Do you think your personal preferences would impact the way you teach ELLs?

No, I don't think it would impact the way I teach ELLs since I understand that all students have different learning preferences. I don't exclusively use strategies that work for me – I also use ones that don't work for me but that I know have worked for others.
