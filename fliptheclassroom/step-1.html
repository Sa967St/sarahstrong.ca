Step 1 - Acquire software and hardware

Drawing Tablet

If you are drawing or writing a lot during your lesson, you may want a drawing tablet instead of a mouse.
Wacom Bamboo is the most well known brand of drawing tablets. They come with digital pens with motion censors.
There are a variety of different models, and the cheapest ones can cost about $50.
They are the drawing tablets used for Khan Academy videos.

Microphone

You will need a microphone if your videos have narration.
You may want to avoid using your computer's microphone because it will pick up unwanted sounds such as your computer's fan running.

Webcam

If you are narrating your videos, you may want to record yourself talking and insert in a corner of your video.
If your computer doesn't have a webcam, any cheap one should suffice, unless you are looking for a high quality one.

Drawing Software

You may need drawing software to write and draw on during your lesson.

You can use the Ink Annotation feature of PowerPoint to write and draw on the slides.
You do not need to have the desktop version of Office installed -- you can use the online version for free.
SmoothDraw is a free versatile drawing software. It is the one used in Khan Academy videos.
You can download SmoothDraw here: http://www.smoothdraw.com/sd.

Screen Capture and Voice Recording Software

You will need software to capture both your computer screen and your voice.

If you deliver your digital lesson using PowerPoint, you can use the built-in recording tool.
TinyTake is a free screen capture software with a basic video editor.
You can capture your screen and audio then add text and drawings direcltly onto parts of the video.
You can download TinyTake here: https://tinytake.com/tinytake-download.
Camtasia Studio is a screen capture software with a powerful video editor.
The cheapest full version is over $100, although you an download a free trial for 30 days.
It is the screen capture software used for Khan Academy videos.
You can download Camtasia Studio here: https://www.techsmith.com/camtasia.html.

Video Editing Software

If you don't intent on doing any post-production editing, except for maybe trimming the video and inserting video clips, all you will need is a basic free video editing software.

All PCs come with Windows Movie Maker installed, and all Macs come with iMovie installed, and either of those should suffice for very basic editing.

If you want to be able to do more editing your videos and you do not have Camtasia Studio, you may want to look into the following non-free options.

Adobe Premier Elements is a basic video editing program with more features than Windows Movie Maker. It can cost as low as $70.
It is listed as a Digital Learning Resource on OSAPAC, so educators can get an older version of it for free.
You can download Adobe Premier Elements here: http://www.adobe.com/products/premiere-elements.html.
Adobe Premier Pro is the enhanced version of Adobe Premier Elements. It costs $240 for a one-year subscription.
You can download Adobe Premier Pro here: http://www.adobe.com/products/premiere.html.
Cyberlink PowerDirector is a powerful video editing program that is known for its fast, high-quality rendering. It can cost as low as $60.
You can download Cyberlink PowerDirector here: http://www.cyberlink.com/products/powerdirector-ultra/features_en_CA.html.
