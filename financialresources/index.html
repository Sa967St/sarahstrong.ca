Financial Resources

Thinking about going to university or college? Learn about the financial options available to you so you can save money!


Scholarships, Bursaries, and Grants

A scholarships, bursaries and grants are money awarded based on high grades (usually the average of your top six grade 12 U or M courses) and/or financial need. All universities offer financial options such as these. It's important to be aware of them because many of them require you to apply for them by a strict deadline that is not necessarily near the end of the school year.

Here are some examples that are not dependent on the program you are applying for.

Loans

You can apply for loans to pay for the costs associated with post-secondary education. After you finish your studies, you can slowly pay back your loans at an affordable rate.

  • The most common way students in Ontatio get loans is through Ontario Student Assistance Program, abbreviated OSAP for short. Click here to learn more about OSAP.

  • Banks and credit unions offer loans called lines of credit/. Contact your financial institution to see if you are eligible for a line of credit
